Cookie Policy

Last updated: January 1, 2026

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to the website owners. Cookies help us provide you with a better experience by enabling us to monitor which pages you find useful and which you do not.

How We Use Cookies

TechPulse uses cookies in a range of ways to improve your experience on our website, including:

  • Understanding how you use our website
  • Remembering your preferences and settings
  • Keeping you signed in (if applicable)
  • Delivering relevant advertising
  • Analyzing website traffic and user behavior
  • Improving website performance and functionality

Types of Cookies We Use

1. Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable core functionality such as security, network management, and accessibility. You may disable these by changing your browser settings, but this may affect how the website functions.

Examples of essential cookies we use:

  • Session cookies to maintain your browsing session
  • Security cookies to protect user data
  • Load balancing cookies to ensure smooth website operation

2. Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. This helps us improve our website structure and content.

We use the following analytics services:

  • Google Analytics: Helps us analyze website traffic and user behavior. Google Analytics cookies collect information such as how you found our site, which pages you visit, and how long you spend on each page.

3. Advertising Cookies

These cookies are used to make advertising messages more relevant to you and your interests. They also perform functions like preventing the same ad from continuously reappearing and ensuring that ads are properly displayed.

We work with the following advertising partners:

  • Google AdSense: Uses cookies to serve personalized ads based on your interests and browsing history.

4. Functionality Cookies

These cookies enable the website to provide enhanced functionality and personalization. They may be set by us or by third-party providers whose services we have added to our pages.

Examples include:

  • Remembering your language preferences
  • Storing your display preferences
  • Remembering if you've accepted our cookie policy

5. Social Media Cookies

These cookies are set by social media services that we have added to the site to enable you to share our content with your friends and networks. They can track your browser across other sites and build a profile of your interests.

Third-Party Cookies

In addition to our own cookies, we may also use various third-party cookies to report usage statistics of the Service, deliver advertisements on and through the Service, and so on. These third-party services include:

  • Google Analytics
  • Google AdSense
  • Social media platforms (Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn)

Please note that third parties (including advertising networks and providers of external services like web traffic analysis services) may also use cookies, over which we have no control. These cookies are likely to be analytical/performance cookies or targeting cookies.

Managing Your Cookie Preferences

Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings preferences. To find out more about cookies, including how to see what cookies have been set and how to manage and delete them, visit www.allaboutcookies.org.

Here are links to cookie management instructions for popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Internet Explorer
  • Microsoft Edge

Opting Out of Targeted Advertising

You can opt out of targeted advertising by visiting these opt-out pages:

  • Google Ads Settings
  • Digital Advertising Alliance
  • Your Online Choices (EU)

Do Not Track

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" feature that signals to websites that you visit that you do not want to have your online activity tracked. Our website responds to Do Not Track signals and will not track your browsing activity when this feature is enabled in your browser.

What Happens If You Disable Cookies

You can choose to disable cookies through your individual browser options. However, if you choose to refuse cookies, you may not be able to use some portions of our Site. Specifically:

  • You may not be able to sign in or use authentication features
  • Your preferences may not be remembered
  • Some content and features may not work properly
  • You may see less relevant advertisements

Changes to This Cookie Policy

We may update our Cookie Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Cookie Policy on this page and updating the "Last updated" date. You are advised to review this Cookie Policy periodically for any changes.
