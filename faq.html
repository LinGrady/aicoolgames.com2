AI & Machine Learning

What is Artificial Intelligence (AI)?

Artificial Intelligence refers to computer systems designed to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as visual perception, speech recognition, decision-making, and language translation. Modern AI uses machine learning algorithms to improve performance over time based on data and experience.

AI is transforming industries from healthcare and finance to transportation and entertainment. Our AI Revolution article provides a comprehensive overview of current AI developments.

What is the difference between AI and Machine Learning?

Machine Learning is a subset of AI that focuses on algorithms that can learn from data without being explicitly programmed. Think of AI as the broad field and machine learning as a specific technique within that field. Other AI approaches include rule-based systems, expert systems, and symbolic AI.

What are AI Agents?

AI Agents are autonomous AI systems that can plan, execute, and complete tasks with minimal human oversight. Unlike traditional AI tools that simply respond to prompts, AI agents can use tools, browse the web, write code, and coordinate multiple steps to achieve complex goals. Read our detailed guide on AI Agents in 2026.

What is GPT-5?

GPT-5 is OpenAI's latest large language model, released in early 2026. It features dramatically improved reasoning capabilities, extended context windows (up to 2 million tokens), and enhanced multimodal understanding across text, images, audio, and video. Learn more in our OpenAI and ChatGPT 2026 article.

What is Claude AI?

Claude is Anthropic's AI assistant, known for its strong safety features and Constitutional AI framework. Claude 4, released in late 2025, demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on scientific and mathematical benchmarks while maintaining industry-leading harmlessness scores. Read our Claude AI analysis for more details.

Apple Intelligence is Apple's comprehensive AI platform for iPhone, iPad, and Mac. What is 5G and why does it matter?

5G is the fifth generation of mobile network technology, offering significantly faster speeds, lower latency, and greater capacity than 4G. It enables applications like autonomous vehicles, remote surgery, and massive IoT deployments that weren't possible with previous generations.
